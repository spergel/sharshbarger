Sam Harshbarger

Doctoral Student at Oxford

Editor · Essayist · Critic

I am a sixth-year doctoral student in History of Science and Medicine at Yale University. I have a prior Ph.D. in Developmental Biology and an M.A. in Conceptual and Historical Studies of Science from the University of Chicago.

My current work is at the intersection of global, environmental, and intellectual history. My dissertation narrates how contingent human interactions in the 20th century constructed the "reef" in Belize, Sri Lanka, the Gulf of Mexico, and the Philippines in both local and global ways.
