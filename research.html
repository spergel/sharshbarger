Current Research

My doctoral research examines the historical development of Azerbaijan's oil industry and its broader implications for the Middle East. I focus on how the exploitation of Azeri oil resources shaped regional politics, international relations, and local communities during the 20th century.

Research Areas

  • History of Oil in the Middle East
  • Azerbaijan's Industrial Development
  • Energy Politics and International Relations
  • Environmental History of the Caspian Region

Dissertation Project

My dissertation, "Black Gold and the Black Sea: Azerbaijan's Oil Industry and Middle Eastern Politics," explores how the development of Baku's oil fields influenced regional power dynamics and shaped modern Middle Eastern history. Through extensive archival research in multiple countries, I examine the intersection of industrial development, environmental change, and political transformation in the Caspian region.
